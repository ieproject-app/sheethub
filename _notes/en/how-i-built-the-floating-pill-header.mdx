The header is the "face" of a website. For SnipGeek, I wanted something that felt lightweight, high-tech, and non-intrusive. That's why I chose the **Floating Pill Header** design.

## The Design Philosophy

Most websites use a top-fixed bar that spans the full width. While functional, it often feels "heavy." A floating pill, on the other hand, creates a sense of depth. By using `backdrop-blur-md` and a slightly transparent background, the header looks like a piece of frosted glass hovering over the content.

## Precision Tech Update

Recently, SnipGeek underwent a major visual transition. We moved away from very soft rounded corners to a more technical **"Precision Tech"** standard with a consistent **4px radius** (`rounded-lg`) across all cards and images.

However, if you look at the header, it remains a **Pill** (full rounded). This was a conscious decision. The contrast between the sharp 4px corners of the content cards and the smooth, perfect curves of the header makes the navigation bar stand out. It reinforces the idea that the header is a specialized tool floating above the technical documentation below.

## Technical Implementation

The magic happens with a few key Tailwind classes:
- `fixed top-4`: Keeps it at the top with a bit of breathing room.
- `rounded-full`: Gives it the signature pill shape.
- `backdrop-blur-sm`: Ensures readability even when scrolling over complex text or images.
- `z-50`: Keeps it above everything else.

By combining these with a scroll-hide logic, the header becomes a dynamic element that reacts to the user's behavior, disappearing when they focus on reading and reappearing when they need to navigate.
